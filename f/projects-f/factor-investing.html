Quantitative Equity Strategy

Long-Short Factor
Investing Strategy

A market-neutral long-short equity strategy built on three academically-grounded risk premia — Value, Momentum, and Quality — constructed from scratch on the S&P 500 universe using MSCI-aligned composite scoring, sector-neutral z-scores, and Betting-Against-Beta (BAB) portfolio construction. Each factor is validated independently and combined into a multi-factor portfolio benchmarked against the S&P 500, with monthly rebalancing.

⚠ Research disclaimer. All results are backtested on historical data. No transaction costs, borrowing costs, or market impact are modelled. Results are not indicative of future performance and do not constitute investment advice.

Methodology
Why these factors persist

Factor premia are explained by a combination of risk-based and behavioural arguments. Risk-based theories hold that higher expected returns compensate investors for bearing systematic risks that cannot be diversified away. Behavioural theories argue that cognitive biases — overreaction, anchoring, herding — create persistent mispricings that rational investors can exploit.

  • Value: Cheap stocks carry higher fundamental risk. Investors systematically overprice glamour stocks and underprice value stocks due to extrapolation bias.
  • Momentum: Underreaction to news causes prices to drift in the direction of recent returns. Trend-following and disposition effects reinforce the anomaly.
  • Quality: Investors underweight the persistence of high-quality earnings. High profitability is not fully reflected in prices due to limited attention and accounting complexity.
Score construction
  • Value Score = avg[z(−P/B), z(−P/E), z(DY)] — following MSCI Global Factor Index methodology
  • Momentum Score = avg[z(12M ret), z(6M ret), z(3M ret), z(dist MA200), z(dist VWAP)]
  • Quality Score = avg[z(ROE), z(ROA), z(Gross Profit/Assets), z(FCF/Assets), z(CF/NI), z(−Accruals)]
  • All z-scores are sector-neutral — computed within GICS sector × date groups to avoid sector loading
  • All metrics winsorised at 1st/99th percentile before scoring to remove outlier contamination
General Score Formula Scorei,t = (1/N) Σ zsector(winsorise(xi,t))
Data sources
  • Price data: EODHD API — adjusted daily close, volume, high/low for all S&P 500 constituents. Stored in HDF5 format keyed by ticker.
  • Fundamentals: EODHD quarterly statements — Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow. Filing dates used to shift signals to the next trading day after announcement.
  • Dividend Yield: EODHD dividend history — annualised DPS / price, recomputed daily.
  • S&P 500 benchmark: daily index returns 1976–2026, compounded to monthly for comparison.
Universe construction
  • Starting universe: all historical S&P 500 constituents from 2005 to 2026, sourced from Wikipedia revision history to approximate index membership at each date.
  • Liquidity filter: stocks with 30-day average dollar volume below the 20th percentile of the universe are excluded at each rebalance.
  • Survivorship bias: delisted tickers are included up to their last available date; no forward-looking exclusion.
  • Minimum history: stocks require at least 252 trading days of price history for beta estimation.
Long-short construction
  • At each month-end, stocks ranked by composite factor score within the eligible universe.
  • Long leg: top 20% of scores (high factor exposure). Short leg: bottom 20% (low factor exposure).
  • Equal weighting within each leg — no size or liquidity tilt applied beyond the initial filter.
  • Multi-factor portfolio: equal-weighted average of Value, Momentum, and Quality monthly returns.
Beta-neutralisation (BAB)

Following Frazzini & Pedersen (2014), each leg is scaled by the inverse of its ex-ante beta to achieve beta neutrality with respect to the market:

BAB Return Formula rBAB = (1/βL) · rL − (1/βH) · rH
  • Rolling 252-day beta estimated as Cov(ri, rm) / Var(rm) for each stock at each rebalance date.
  • Beta bounds: [0.05, 3.0] — extreme betas excluded.
Validation checks performed
  • Stock counts per leg monitored each month — portfolios with fewer than 150 names excluded.
  • Return sanity checks: extreme monthly returns (|r| > 50%) investigated and traced to specific stocks/events.
  • Look-ahead bias: fundamentals shifted to next trading day after filing date; momentum signals skip most-recent month (t−1).
  • Duplicate row checks performed on all HDF5 data layers before any portfolio construction.
Known limitations
  • No transaction costs. High-turnover factors (Momentum) are most affected. Real-world implementation would require netting trades across factors.
  • No borrowing costs. Short-selling feasibility and locate costs are not modelled.
  • Equal weights within legs. Size and liquidity biases are not controlled beyond the dollar-volume filter.
  • Dividend Yield data starts 2005. F_DY sub-factor history is limited; Value score falls back to P/B and P/E only for tickers with missing DY.
Key References
Literature
Fama, E. F., & French, K. R. (1992)
The Cross-Section of Expected Stock Returns. The Journal of Finance, 47(2), 427–465.
The foundational paper establishing that size and book-to-market ratio explain the cross-section of expected returns — the direct academic basis for the Value factor.
Jegadeesh, N., & Titman, S. (1993)
Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers. The Journal of Finance, 48(1), 65–91.
The seminal paper establishing price momentum as a persistent anomaly. Documents that stocks with strong 3–12 month past returns continue to outperform — the core motivation for the Momentum factor.
Frazzini, A., & Pedersen, L. H. (2014)
Betting Against Beta. Journal of Financial Economics, 111(1), 1–23.
Introduces the BAB factor and the leverage-constrained investor framework. The BAB scaling methodology used in this project to construct beta-neutral portfolios follows directly from this paper.
Asness, C. S., Frazzini, A., & Pedersen, L. H. (2019)
Quality Minus Junk. Review of Accounting Studies, 24(1), 34–112.
Defines and validates the Quality factor using profitability, growth, safety, and payout metrics. The composite scoring approach — ROE, ROA, accruals, FCF — is closely aligned with the QMJ framework from this paper.
MSCI Inc. (2021)
MSCI Global Investable Market Indexes Methodology. MSCI Index Methodology Documentation, February 2021.
The official MSCI documentation for factor index construction — sector-neutral z-scoring, winsorisation, and composite score aggregation. The scoring methodology in this project follows the MSCI specification as closely as available data permits.
